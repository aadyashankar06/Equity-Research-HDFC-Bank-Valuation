# HDFC Bank Equity Research & Valuation

## Overview
This project presents a comprehensive equity research analysis of HDFC Bank, one of India’s largest private sector banks. The study evaluates the bank’s financial performance, merger impact, valuation, and future growth potential, and develops a structured investment thesis based on data-driven insights.

## Problem Statement
- Post-merger integration with HDFC Ltd has created structural changes in the balance sheet
- Compression in Net Interest Margins (NIM) affecting profitability
- Governance uncertainty due to leadership transition
- Market undervaluation despite strong fundamentals

## Key Insights
- NIM declined to ~3.35% post-merger due to high-cost borrowings
- Loan-to-Deposit Ratio peaked at ~110% and is now normalizing
- Strong asset quality with GNPA ~1.24%
- Significant cross-selling opportunity from HDFC Ltd customer base
- Bank is trading at a valuation discount compared to historical averages

## Approach
- Financial Analysis
- Revenue and profitability trends (NII, PAT)
- Asset quality (GNPA, NNPA)
- Efficiency metrics (ROA, ROE, Cost-to-Income)
- Funding strength (CASA ratio)

## Valuation
- Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)
- Forecasted earnings growth
- Cost of equity and terminal value assumptions
- Intrinsic value estimation
- Relative Valuation
- Peer comparison with ICICI Bank, Axis Bank, and Kotak Mahindra Bank
- P/E and P/B multiple analysis

## Risk Assessment
- Delayed NIM recovery due to deposit competition
- Governance and leadership uncertainty
- Macroeconomic risks (interest rates, inflation, oil prices)
- Competitive pressure from fintech and digital platforms

## Scenario Analysis
- Bull Case: NIM recovery and valuation re-rating
- Base Case: Gradual normalization of margins
- Bear Case: Continued margin pressure and governance risks
  
## Impact
- Identifies a 50–70% upside potential based on valuation models
- Provides a structured investment thesis for long-term investors
- Highlights key catalysts for re-rating and growth
- Evaluates downside risk with limited (~10%) downside scenario
